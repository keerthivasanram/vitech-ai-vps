2. What we have built (prototype)

Specification engine

Generates a technical spec for a new requirement by adapting your closest past projects, refined by engineering rules. Each value is tagged with its source and a confidence score.

Two expert modes, one engine

Consulting Engineer designs from engineering knowledge; ATS Engineering Expert builds from your historical offers. Same system, you choose the source of truth.

Quotation agent

Budgetary price derived from your offers (nearest project, scaled by size, cross-checked against a price trend), plus scope of supply and standard commercial terms — as a reviewable draft.

Grounded knowledge base

Loaded with 33 real Vitech offers across 10 equipment types. Exact analytics (counts, lists, clients) are computed in code, and general engineering questions are answered conversationally.

3. How it works

Every request flows through the same pipeline:

Understand the request  →  Retrieve similar past projects  →  Reason (engineering rules + case-based adaptation from your data)  →  Validate & score confidence  →  Explain in plain language.

The guiding principle: the AI writes the words; engineering rules and your real offers write every number. A diagram of the full architecture is included separately (architecture.html).

6. What we need from you

To build the next phase well, we need data and a few decisions. These are the discussion points for the meeting:

Historical data (most important). Can you share a larger set of past offers/quotations? Roughly how many do you have, and in what form (a shared folder, an ERP export, email attachments)? More real data directly improves accuracy.
File formats — offers. Are your offer documents native/digital PDFs (text you can select) or scanned images? This decides whether we can read them directly or need image recognition (OCR).
File formats — CAD. Which formats do you work in — DWG, DXF, or PDF/image exports? Do drawings use a standard title-block template? This decides how reliably we can read dimensions and details.
Priority equipment. Which product lines should we cover first (wet scrubbers, paint/powder booths, dust collectors, ovens…)? We tune the engineering rules per category.
Pricing & costing. Do you have a rate card or component-cost data? Today we estimate at system level; a rate card would let us cost line-by-line. Also: standard margin and rounding conventions.
Commercial terms & branding. Your standard validity, payment, delivery and warranty wording, plus letterhead details (logo, address, GST) so quotations print in your official format.
Workflow & approvals. Who prepares and who approves a spec/quotation today? Where should the assistant fit, and who is the reviewer before anything is issued?
Deployment & privacy. Should this run on your own hardware/on-premise (data stays in-house) or in the cloud? Any hardware available (e.g. a GPU machine)?
Scale. Roughly how many quotations/specs per week, and how many users? This sizes the system.
Success criteria. What would make this a clear win for you in the first phase (time saved per quote, consistency, fewer errors)? We will measure against it.
